Annie Stephenson

I am a Research Scientist in the LLM Psychology and Sociology team in the Alignment of Complex Systems Research Group (ACS). Previously, I was a Postdoctoral researcher at Princeton University, supervised by Prof. Simon Levin in the department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology. I received my PhD in Applied Physics from Harvard University in Prof. Vinny Manoharan’s group, where I was supported by the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship Program (GRFP). I completed my undergraduate degree in physics at the University of Notre Dame.

My PhD work was centered on structural color, a type of color that comes from light's interaction with nanoscale structures instead of from pigments. This phenomenon is present in coloration throughout nature, from bird feathers and butterfly wings to plants and opals. I enjoyed the day-to-day process of making samples, measuring their optical properties, and building models to make sense of the data. I liked finding reproducible, yet unexplained signatures in data, and hunting for a testable explanation.

During my graduate studies, I was drawn to topics with broad applications, such as self-organization and the characterization of order and disorder. I eventually realized that the questions that motivated me were outside of traditional physics; I decided to pivot to study human collective behavior for my postdoc, and through that work became interested in how AI is shifting our social systems. After completing the PIBBSS fellowship, where I was able to begin working on AI safety, I started my current role at ACS where I'm doing experimental work to understand LLMs from a psychological and sociological perspective.
