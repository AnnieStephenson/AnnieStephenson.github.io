Annie Stephenson

Comparing emergent social behavior of humans and AI agents


How do emergent social behaviors of AI agents compare to those of human groups? This project explores that question by simulating group dynamics in structured social environments using a fully text-based version of the roleplaying game Dungeons & Dragons (D&D). Using transcripts from hundreds of human games, I analyze activity patterns and emergent creativity and group cohesion. I then compare these with the behaviors of LLM agents placed in equivalent gameplay settings, revealing where their social instincts align with or diverge from those of humans. The goal is to understand how our social systems may change as we integrate more AI agents, and to develop metrics that could help evaluate these changes in real-world contexts.
