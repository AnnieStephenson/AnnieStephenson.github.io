Annie Stephenson

My core questions include:

  • What mechanisms cause human collectives to show reproducible emergent behaviors, even when each person makes choices based on individual experiences?
  • What environmental factors or organizational strategies foster cooperation, coordination, and collective intelligence?
  • How can AI systems help us understand emergent behaviors in human collectives, and how can these insights shape the design of cooperative AI agents that exhibit collective intelligence?

In my PhD work on light-matter interactions in disordered systems, I relied on Maxwell's equations, boundary conditions, and established approximations as foundations for more complex theories. Such solid “ground truths” are not established in the study of human behavior since our actions depend strongly on our unique circumstances and historical conditions. Still, patterns do emerge in human data, such as burstiness in communication, scaling laws in organizations, and social network features, suggesting there are some reliable trends, at least on average. My current projects use a combination of mechanistic modeling and machine learning to understand behavior observed in online social systems. Machine learning reveals and recreates complex patterns in data that drive collective behavior, while agent-based and dynamical systems models enable generalizable insights about the mechanisms that drive these behaviors.
